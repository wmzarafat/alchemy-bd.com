Development Process

Requirement analysis is a key component in the software development lifecycle and is usually the initial step before the project commences. It is the process of determining user expectations for a new or modified product which involves frequent communication with system users to determine specific feature expectations, resolution of conflict or ambiguity in requirements as demanded by the various users or groups of users, avoidance of feature creep and documentation of all aspects of the project development process from start to finish. Requirements analysis is a team effort that demands a combination of hardware, software and human factors engineering expertise as well as skills in dealing with people.


In this stage the proposed system and software design is prepared from the requirement specifications which were gathered in the requirement analysis part. The System Analyst design the logical part of the project for the designers and designer gets the basic idea of designing the software design front end and back end both. System Design helps in specifying hardware and system requirements and also helps in defining overall system architecture. The system design specifications serve as input for the next phase of the model.


Our experienced developers will then take your design, divide in modules/units and bring it to life, making it robust and functional with clean, high-quality code. This is the longest phase of the software development life cycle. During this and every phase, you’ll be given progress reports and be in close communication with us to ensure an expedited project.


Our professional team does test against the requirements to make sure that the product is actually solving the needs addressed and gathered during the requirements phase after the code is developed. During this phase unit testing, integration testing, system testing, acceptance testing are done by our relevant personnel’s.


After successful quality testing the product is delivered / deployed to the customer for their use. The user impact of bugs is obvious; less obvious is that the earlier we identify bugs, the easier and cheaper they are to fix. We have confidence that it will work smoothly and seamlessly.


Software maintenance is a very broad activity that includes error correction, enhancements of capabilities, deletion of obsolete capabilities, and optimization. Because change is inevitable, mechanisms must be developed for evaluation, controlling and making modifications. The purpose is to preserve the value of software over the time. The value can be enhanced by expanding the customer base, meeting additional requirements, becoming easier to use, more efficient and employing newer technology.
